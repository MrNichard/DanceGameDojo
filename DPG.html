Welcome to the dance pad guide

Here I'll take you through the various kinds and types of dance pads, and provide the needed information about them. If you'd like a more detailed explanation of the various types of pads, please check out this video by Dom_ITG or the Dance Pad Guide on R/DanceDanceRevolution

Arcade Pads

Arcade pads are the real deal. These pads were made for the public arcade enviornment, so they were built to last. Doesn't matter if it's from an ITG, DDR, or PIU cab. That said, the quality you get/find them in may vary. They may have dead sensors, dim/dead lights, or panels covered in rust. Thankfully, sites like Mysticsteps and DDRpad.com sell parts so you can fix up and even upgrade your dancepad. Pricing also varies, but they are usually around the $400-$1000 mark (depending on the pad type and quality). I myself own an Arcade pad (1st gen DDR JCab pad), and can say that it has held up well.

Stepmaniax

Stepmaniax pads are arcade pads you can buy brand new. They're made by Kyle Ward, the same person who helped to create the In The Groove series of Dance Games. Instead of arcade sensors, these pads use FSR's.

L-tek

L-Tek pads are essentially the modern day Cobalt Fluxes. I used one of these for around 3 months, and can say that it's pretty good.
