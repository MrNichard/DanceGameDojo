Welcome to About Me

This page will go into detail on me and my history with dance games. (Some of the pictures are placeholders by the way, and those photos and the layout will be updated/fixed as time goes on. )

Lets start from the beginning, or rather 2018 (I think). This new arcade in Westfield opened up probably a month before, and me and my group of friends felt like checking it out. While there we saw that they had a DDR cabinet, and so we decided we'd give it a whirl. Now I don't think any of use took it seriously, but I remember finding myself enamored with the game. Everytime I went back, it was always the first thing I played. I lost count on the amount of times I played Trust on that cab, and I still play it every once in a while. That being said, I really wasn't taking any other steps to actually improve.

Yestercades DDR SN2 cab (older picture, but it still looks like this)

About a year after I discovered dance games, I had actually made some progress. Then the Covid pandemic came around, and that prevented me from going to the arcade for the time being. Thankfully, there was a solution to this. Back in the 2000's, Konami released a bunch of home version of there DDR games for home consoles, which included the PS2. The best part was my neightbor had given me his old PS2 about a month prior, so all I had to do was buy the games and a pad. If I remember correctly the games I got were DDRMAX & Supernova, and I bought two cheap softpads to go with them (one of them didn't even work when they arrived). I still have the games, but I got rid of the softpads years ago.

The first DDR game I ever owned.

At the start of 10th grade, I discovered an arcade (thanks to my Japanese Teacher) that would basically become a second home to me. In Freshman year I learned that my Japanese teacher also played Dance games. Me being me, I asked her a good amount of question on the subject. I think eventually the topic of arcades came up, and she mentioned an arcade about half an hour away that had a bunch of rhythm games. After hearing that, I just had to go. Since heraing about the arcade (which I found out was called 8 on the break), I've probably been there at least a hunderd times.

Outside of 8otb (older picture)

Like yestercades, I just had to go back to 8otb when the chance came up. On the 5th of 6th visit, I ended up meeting my first dance game friend, Shayne. I haven't seen him since september, but I still do keep in contact with him via text. He's been busy with wrestling & other sports, so I haven't had the chance to meet up with him in person. Around a year after I first discovered the place, I began going there ever Friday or Saturday. A couple weeks after I started that, I met another friend (Nate/LegendaryN8) that I still meet up with every Saturday. Funny enough, he's actually friends with my Japanese teacher (small world). Nate is probably the reason that I decided to step up my game and begin improving.

Outside of 8otb (older picture)

Shortly after meeting Nate is when I really began to take strides in improving my accuracy & Stamina. I began branching out to songs outside of officials, and began playing longer songs with more streams (stamina). This turned into a routine where I would come most saturdays of the month, and stay for about 2-3 hours. At the time I still couldn't drive, at least not without a parent, so my mom or dad would usually have to tag along. Eventually I began to see some actual improvement in my skillset & what I could play. To keep up this improvement, I ended up purchasing an L-Tek dance pad to play ITG/Stepmania at home. Although it worked pretty good, I did feel like I needed a bit more.

I had this for about 3 months, then sold it to a friend

Around April of 2022, I stumbled upon a man on Facebook who parts out old DDR cabinets and sells the pad individually. After confirming with some people that he was a reputable source, I sent him a message. It took him a couple of days to respond, but he eventually got back to my inquiry. It took about a day or two to finalize everything, but soon the payment was finalized (though I did haggle down the price a bit). I think it took about 3 or 4 days for it to get from North Carolina (where the guy is based out of) to New Jersey, and even then I had to go out to South Plainfield to pick it up at the drop off center (it was shipped via a trucking company). I did need to fix it up a bit, but other than that I was ecstatic to have something like this.

A picture of what my DDR looked like when I first got it.

When I received the dance pad, I will say it wasn't perfect. With the things having been in an arcade for what was probably decades, it needed a decent amount of TLC. I took removed almost all of the metal panels, and vacuumed up all the dirt that accumulated in the pads. That took me about 20 minutes, and then it was on to the other stuff. I needed to remove all the old tape and foam stuff from the L-Brackets to replace it with gorilla tape. I ended up using goo gone and some other stuff to remove it all, and that took about 2 hours. Then I had to spent some time testing the sensors to see which ones were the most & least sensitive. That way I could see where I could put them on each section of the panel.

Outside of 8otb (older picture)

With that, this basically brings us to the present day. Since buying the arcade pad, my accuracy has increase substantially (same for my Stamina). Currently I can get 99%+ on most songs from blocks 9-11 on expert, and I'm starting to work on doing the same for 12's. Regarding Stamina, I can pass up to 14's up to 180bpm. I am trying to break into passing both 15 and higher, as well as faster stuff bpm wise. I've also been to my first tournament in the time I got the pad. I didn't get far, but I still had a good time. Other than some minor improvements in my skillset, can't really say much had happened. Doesn't mean things can't change, and if anything does it will be added to this page in a timely manor.
