Welcome to Gametypes and Versions

Here I'll you take you through the various type of dance games released. and show you the various versions of said games.

4 Panel

Games that have a layout using 4 arrow panels

Dance Dance Revolution (DDR), is a 4 panel game made by Konami; starting back in 1998. Besides being the most common dance game, it is also the most well known Dance game across the general public. It has been areound for 25 years after all.

A modern day DDR cab looks like this.

ITG was a 4 panel game made by the American company Roxor between 2004 & 2006. Created to fill the void in DDR's abscense, it contained songs that were at the time, much harder than anything that DDR had.

An ITG dedicab looks like this

5 Panel

Games that have a layout using 5 arrow panels

Hitting the arcade floors a year after DDR became avaliable, PIU is a 5 panel dance game made by Korean Company Andamiro. Besides the panel layout being very different from DDR, the song selection also has a noticeable change; having a good chunk of the song list being K-pop or Korean oriented songs.

This is what a modern PIU cabinet looks like.

Stepmaniax is the newest dance game on the market, and is quickly becoming more common as new machines find there way into arcades. SMX uses the same arrow layout as DDR and ITG, only with the center panel also being active.

This is what an SMX cabinet looks like.

6 panel

Games that have a layout using 6 arrow panels

DDR Solo is unique, as it is (to my knowledge) the only dance game to use 6 panels during gameplay. This version of DDR was only made between 1999 and 2000, and is not the most common version of the game you'll find.

Here is an example of one.

9 Panel

Games that have a layout using 9 arrow panels

Rhythm Horizon is an up and coming 9 panel dance game, created by veteran players Matt (Mad Matt/Sigatrev) & Lara (Hurtpiggypig). It is still in Beta at the moment, however you can actually purchase said beta for a monthly fee. To go along with this game, they are creating there own pad that uses FSR's. Unfortunately, like the full game, it's far from being finished.

Werdwerdus playing the beta.

Technomotion was a short lived 9 panel game created by the Korean company F2 systems. The pads were in the style of DDR, but there is an arrow panel on each section of the pad. The cabinet itself looks to be a modified design of a DDR cabinet, but I cannot tell for sure. The game allowed you to pick a 4, 5, or 8 panel mode.

This is what it looks like. .

Other

Games using other types of arrow panel layouts

Ez2dancer was made by Korean Company Amuseworld between 2000 and 2004. The game is unique, as it uses both hand and foot sensors. It uses 3 foot sensors (in a kinda oval shape), and two infrared hand sensors. It is a rarity to find one of these nowadays, as the only really known cabinets were in Chile, the U.K, the Phillipines, and Korea.

Here is a picture of one.

While it may not look like it has panels, the whole platform is essentially one big panel. During gameplay there is 4 visible markers on the screen, and you need to shuffle your feet across the platform to hit the notes that come down said markers.
