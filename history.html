Tournaments

DDR STORM began in the year 2001 as a small DDR tournament in Florida. Over the years, they expanded to adding in games like PIU & ITG.

RMT began in the early 2000's as a DDR tournament held in North Carolina, held at Putt Indoor Mini Golf & Games until the arcade closes down.

R.I.P has been held at the University of Illinois since 2010, holding tournaments for FA/Tech charts as well as Mod Charts.

Held in a few places across Europe, the ITG Eurocup was a tournament in which players from all across Europe would come together to compete. Starting as the World Cup in 2006, it would then become the Eurocup in 2008, being held at Silverstone in the Netherlands until 2010.

D.O.W.N.S, U.P.S, and now R.I.G.H.T.S are a series of tournaments focuses primarily around mod charts. The first one was held at Tropical Fun Zone, where it then moved to Harrisburg FFC, and then to Penn State.

Starting in 2007 as Kings of the coast, Champions of the coast has been held at 8 on the break in Dunellen, NJ on a yearly bases. The tournaments have been put on hiatus since the covid pandemic hit, but there are hope that it will be making a comeback.

The East Coast series has been around since the early 2010's, with ECS being the only one to be fully in person at one point. As the names would suggest, ECS is focused on Stamina, while ECFA is focuses on Foot Accuracy. In recent times ECFA has gone under a new name, ITL, and will continue to go by this in the near future.

Game History

Hitting the Japanese arcades in 1998, and proceeded to take the world by storm. Beginning with DDR 1st mix, more began to trickle in over the next few years, until in 2002 released DDR Extreme. After that, the series went on Hiatus until 2006 with the release of Supernova. After that, Konami began to start releasing new Versions on a fairly regular basis. Currently the lastes version of the game is A3. If you want to play it though, you're going to have to find a Gold cab.
